The World is dying, wether we like it or not. In this
webpage, you will find out what you can do on your
part to keep the world alive.

These are some of the ways other people are saving the earth and what we can do now and in the future.

  1. Vertical Forests

    2. They are simple but EFFECTIVE. All
    you basically have to do is put up a LOT of
    plants in your balcony. Apart from looking really cool,
    vertical forests have many environmental
    benefits. The vegetation can
    absorb carbon dioxide from the air, preventing
    the gas from causing further greenhouse
    warming in the atmosphere.
    But on top of this, plants and
    trees can act to filter airborne particulates
    either by intercepting them or absorbing them
    through pores in the leaf
    surface. This basically means,
    cleaner air. And all this just from your balcony.

    A Vertical Forest

  2. Electric Bike and Scooter sharing schemes

    3. This is even simpler. Don’t
    wait around for an auto
    or a bus. Get yourself a scooter
    or a bike! These scooters and
    bikes are very eco-friendly as they run on
    electricity and they’re much cheaper as
    no one drives you around. You do it yourself.
    And also you can just tap and pay.
    You could use your phone or your credit card to
    use one and once your done
    using them, you can drop them off at your
    final destination for others to use.
    It is simple after all.

    Electric Bike Sharing

  3. Algae Curtains

    4. But two architects have come up with
    the idea of using giant algae-filled
    plastic curtains with the idea
    of placing them on new buildings as
    ‘living walls’.The curtains are
    comprised of a series of tubes which
    contain microscopic algae. The idea
    is that air from ground level will rise
    up through these tubes containing
    the algae. Because algae are plants
    they extract carbon dioxide from the
    air in the process of photosynthesis
    just like all other vegetation.
    But algae has another advantage.
    Scientists have found they not only
    help with carbon dioxide but they also help
    to break down some of the other air
    pollutants generated by a city.
    The designers hope the idea will take off
    and be used on warehouses and
    other buildings where appearance
    isn’t really an important factor.

    Algae Curtains
